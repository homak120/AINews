Research Paper arxiv · Feb 2026

Constitutional AI v3 — self-correction via red-teaming without human labels

Source
anthropic.com
Published
Mar 29, 2026
Read time
~8 min
Type
Research paper
Why It Matters

This work directly addresses one of the costliest bottlenecks in RLHF: human labellers. By enabling the model to generate and filter its own training signal, Anthropic reduces the need for annotated harmful examples without sacrificing safety performance.

The 60% reduction in labelling costs could meaningfully lower the barrier for smaller labs to train safer models — or allow frontier labs to iterate faster on alignment techniques.

Summary

CAI v3 introduces a self-play red-teaming loop where a helper model attempts to elicit harmful outputs, and the primary model learns to refuse them — all within training, without human-in-the-loop annotation at each step.

The paper demonstrates that this technique maintains 96% of supervised safety performance while operating with dramatically fewer labelled examples, and generalises better to out-of-distribution harmful prompts.
